<p><p>The hydrogen car market consists primarily of two types: Hydrogen Internal Combustion Engine Vehicles (HICEVs) and Fuel Cell Electric Vehicles (FCEVs). HICEVs utilize hydrogen as a fuel source, converting it through combustion in a traditional engine, while maintaining some benefits of cleaner emissions compared to gasoline. Conversely, FCEVs deploy hydrogen in a fuel cell to generate electricity, powering an electric motor. Both types aim to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and lower greenhouse gas emissions in the automotive sector.</p></p>
