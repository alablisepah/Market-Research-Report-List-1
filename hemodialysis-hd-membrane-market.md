<p><p>The hemodialysis (HD) membrane market consists of three primary types: unmodified cellulose, modified/regenerated cellulose, and synthetic membranes. Unmodified cellulose membranes offer basic filtration but have limitations in biocompatibility. Modified/regenerated cellulose membranes enhance performance by improving biocompatibility and reducing thrombogenicity, making them more suitable for longer treatments. Synthetic membranes, often made from polymers, provide superior durability and solute removal efficiency, catering to diverse patient needs and improving overall dialysis outcomes. Each type plays a vital role in optimizing hemodialysis treatments.</p></p>
