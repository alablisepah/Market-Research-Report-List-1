<p><p>Oral Guided Bone Regeneration (GTR) Membrane is a specialized barrier membrane primarily used in dental and maxillofacial surgical procedures to facilitate bone regeneration. These membranes support the growth of new bone by preventing the ingress of soft tissue into the defect site, allowing for a more organized healing process and improved structural integrity. Key materials used in GTR membranes include natural and synthetic polymers, which are designed to resorb over time or be removed post-healing.</p><p>The Oral Guided Bone Regeneration (GTR) Membrane Market is anticipated to experience significant growth, driven by the increasing incidence of dental disorders, advancements in dental surgical techniques, and a rising geriatric population needing dental interventions. <p><ul><li>Absorbable Membrane</li><li>Non-absorbable Membrane</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Oral Guided Bone Regeneration (GTR) Membrane market is divided into two main types: absorbable membranes and non-absorbable membranes. Absorbable membranes are designed to gradually decompose and are integrated into the healing process, eliminating the need for surgical removal. Non-absorbable membranes, on the other hand, remain in place until they are surgically removed, offering prolonged support for bone regeneration. Each type serves distinct clinical needs based on factors like healing duration, patient comfort, and specific surgical procedures.</p></p>
