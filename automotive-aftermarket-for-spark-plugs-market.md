<p><p>The automotive aftermarket for spark plugs comprises two main types: hot spark plugs and cold spark plugs. Hot spark plugs have a longer heat range and are designed to maintain a higher temperature, aiding in efficient combustion and reducing the risk of fouling, making them suitable for everyday driving. In contrast, cold spark plugs dissipate heat more quickly, preventing overheating in high-performance or racing applications. Each type serves specific engine requirements, influencing ignition performance and overall engine efficiency.</p></p>
