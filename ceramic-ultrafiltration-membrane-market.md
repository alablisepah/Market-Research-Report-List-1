<p><p>Ceramic ultrafiltration membranes are classified into two main types: flat-sheet membranes and pipe membranes. Flat-sheet membranes consist of a planar structure that offers a large filtration area, making them effective for various applications, such as water purification and wastewater treatment. Pipe membranes, on the other hand, are cylindrical, optimizing flow rates and space efficiency, often used in industrial processes. Both types leverage ceramic materials' durability and enhanced fouling resistance, ensuring long-lasting performance in filtration systems.</p></p>
